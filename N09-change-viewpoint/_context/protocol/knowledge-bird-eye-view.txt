========================================
GOAL
========================================

Generate a precise "Bird's-eye Perspective View" of the architecture presented in the source image.
Treat the building in the image as a completed, constructed reality.
Change the angle of view to this specific new perspective without altering the building's original geometry, materials, or style.
Render the simulation strictly by resetting the Angle of View and the Optical Environment.

========================================
PHOTOGRAPHER'S WORKFLOW
========================================

A professional drone photography technique for creating a dynamic and hyper-realistic Bird's-eye Perspective view of a building, showcasing its roof plane and facades with a natural sense of depth and scale.

POSITIONING AND PERSPECTIVE:
  Position the drone at a high altitude and angle the camera downward (typically between 45 to 60 degrees) relative to the ground.
  Using a wide-angle to standard focal length (24mm to 35mm) introduces natural perspective distortion (vanishing points), allowing the building to recede realistically into its surrounding context.
  This emphasizes the building's three-dimensional volume.

LIGHTING:
  Shoot under crisp, clear daylight or golden hour lighting with strong directional sunlight.
  This lighting casts defined, natural shadows that carve out the architectural geometry, creating a highly tactile, hyper-realistic atmosphere with rich contrast and deep spatial awareness.

RENDERING AND COMPOSITION:
  Render in ultra-high resolution using High Dynamic Range (HDR) techniques.
  Ensure absolute sharpness and photorealistic detail across both highlights and deep shadows.
  Frame the composition in a 16:9 or 4:3 aspect ratio for a cinematic, immersive view.

========================================
IMAGE GENERATION PARAMETERS
========================================

PROMPT_RULES:
  - Simulate a high-altitude drone view at 45-60 degree downward angle
  - Use wide-angle (24mm equivalent) perspective with natural vanishing points
  - Preserve all original geometry, materials, and proportions — no alteration
  - Apply directional sunlight (clear day or golden hour) with defined shadows
  - Render with HDR quality: sharp highlights and deep shadow detail
  - Aspect ratio: 16:9 or 4:3

STYLE_PARAMS:
  rendering_style: hyper-realistic drone photography, HDR
  camera_height: high altitude (drone)
  camera_angle: 45-60 degrees downward tilt
  perspective: wide-angle, natural vanishing points
  lighting: directional sunlight, crisp clear daylight or golden hour, defined shadows

NEGATIVE_PROMPT:
  cartoon, illustration, sketch, watermark, blur,
  ground-level view, flat lighting, overcast, no shadows,
  altered geometry, added elements, fisheye distortion

QUALITY_GATE:
  If generated image does not show roof plane prominently → regenerate once
  If altered building geometry detected → regenerate once
