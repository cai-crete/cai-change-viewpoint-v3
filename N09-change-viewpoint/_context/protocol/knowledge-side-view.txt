Version: v1

========================================
GOAL
========================================

Generate a precise "Side Elevation View" of the architecture presented in the source image.
Treat the building in the image as a completed, constructed reality.
Change the angle of view to this specific new perspective without altering the building's original geometry, materials, or style.
Render the simulation strictly by resetting the Angle of View and the Optical Environment.

========================================
DIRECTION RULE
========================================

SIDE_DIRECTION INPUT:
  right → Target RIGHT side facade (03:00 direction relative to building front at 06:00)
  left  → Target LEFT side facade  (09:00 direction relative to building front at 06:00)

EXECUTION:
  Scan the source image to identify the building front (06:00 reference).
  Rotate the virtual camera to the specified side direction.
  Position camera so sensor plane is perfectly parallel to the target side facade.

========================================
PHOTOGRAPHER'S WORKFLOW
========================================

Manual Entry: Capturing a Distortion-Free Elevation View (Side)
This professional technique creates a perfectly flat, perspective-corrected representation of a building's side facade.

ANGLE RECOGNITION AND POSITIONING:
  Analyze the source image to identify the front facade.
  Based on side_direction input (right/left), determine the target side facade.
  Place the camera so the sensor plane is perfectly parallel to the building's side surface.

PHOTOGRAPHIC FOCUS:
  Document the formal composition of the side facade.
  Concentrate on the building's sense of proportion, the rhythm of modular elements (windows, panels), and pure materiality.
  Represent the architect's two-dimensional design intent with absolute clarity.

LIGHTING:
  Shoot under the soft, even, diffused light of an overcast day.
  This neutral lighting renders textures and colors accurately without shadows.

LENS AND PERSPECTIVE CONTROL:
  The use of a tilt-shift lens is non-negotiable.
  All vertical and horizontal lines must be perfectly parallel to the frame.
  A telephoto lens can also be used to further compress perspective.

========================================
SPECIFICATION
========================================

VIEW_TYPE: Side Elevation View
SHOOTING_INTENT: Records the architect's design intent accurately and objectively like a 2D drawing. Minimizes perspective to emphasize proportion and rhythm of the side facade.
CAMERA: Sony A7R V
LENS: Canon TS-E 50mm f/2.8L MACRO (Standard angle Tilt-Shift Lens)
APERTURE: f/11
ISO: 100
SHUTTER_SPEED: 1/125 sec
OTHER_EQUIPMENT: Tripod

========================================
IMAGE GENERATION PARAMETERS
========================================

PROMPT_RULES:
  - Read side_direction from input: "right" → 03:00 position / "left" → 09:00 position
  - Simulate a perfectly flat, side-facing elevation view — camera sensor parallel to side facade
  - Preserve all original geometry, materials, and proportions — no alteration
  - Apply soft overcast lighting (no harsh shadows obscuring facade)
  - All vertical and horizontal lines must be perfectly straight

STYLE_PARAMS:
  rendering_style: photorealistic architectural elevation photography
  camera_angle: 90-degree side view (either right or left per input)
  perspective: minimal, near-orthographic
  lighting: overcast diffused, flat even illumination
  lens_correction: tilt-shift, all lines parallel

NEGATIVE_PROMPT:
  cartoon, illustration, sketch, watermark, blur,
  perspective distortion, corner view, front view, tilted verticals,
  altered geometry, added elements, harsh shadows

QUALITY_GATE:
  If image shows wrong side (left when right requested, or vice versa) → regenerate once
  If perspective distortion present → regenerate once
  If vertical/horizontal lines are not parallel → regenerate once
